### The Dalio Signal — US Position in the Big Debt Cycle

Dalio's book *How Countries Go Broke: The Big Cycle* (published 2025) uses a framework of **nine stages** of the Big Debt Cycle. Note: this is a different framework from the 7-stage model in his earlier *Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises* (2018). The "Stage 5" assessment below is specific to the *How Countries Go Broke* 9-stage framework.

**His explicit assessment of the US:**

- **"We are now in Stage 5"** (the stage immediately preceding Stage 6: "great disruption/breakdown")
  — Ray Dalio, Fortune, March 14, 2026 → [Fortune, March 14, 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/03/14/ray-dalio-big-cycle-debt-crisis-political-disorder-world-order/)

- **"The US is in the highly dangerous 'fifth stage'"** with disorder "almost certainly" in the next 5-10 years
  — Dalio interview on *How Countries Go Broke* Chinese release, September 2025 → [Global Times, Sep 8, 2025](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202509/1342951.shtml)

**Specific metrics Dalio used:**
- US spending ~$7 trillion/year; US revenue ~$5 trillion/year
- **Overspending rate: 40%** of annual revenue
- Existing debt: **~6x annual income** (gross federal debt to revenue ratio)
- Debt service costs: ~$1 trillion (half the deficit)
- Projected timeline: "debt crisis likely within 3-5 years" (from Sep 2025 interview)
- Source: [Global Times, Sep 8, 2025](https://www.globaltimes.cn/page/202509/1342951.shtml)

### Dalio's Eight Power Indicators

From *Changing World Order* and associated research (Source: [economicprinciples.org](https://economicprinciples.org/downloads/cwo-power-index.pdf) and [Dalio LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/big-cycles-over-last-500-years-ray-dalio)):

1. **Education** (long-leading indicator of rise)
2. **Innovation and technology development**
3. **Competitiveness / cost-competitiveness in global markets**
4. **Economic output** (GDP)
5. **Share of global trade**
6. **Military strength**
7. **Financial center power** (capital markets depth)
8. **Reserve currency status** (long-lagging indicator)

**Causal sequence per Dalio:** Education → Innovation → Trade/Military → Financial center → Reserve currency. Education leads by decades; reserve currency lags by decades.

## Section 5: Currency Stability of Safe Haven Currencies (Historical)

### Swiss Franc (CHF) vs. USD

**1970s stagflation period:**
- USD/CHF moved from approximately **4.3 CHF/USD in 1970 to 1.6 CHF/USD at end of 1979**
- CHF appreciated approximately **+170% against USD** over the decade
- Driven by Switzerland's monetarist policy restraint vs. US inflation
- Source: [SNB CHF History — US Stagflation](https://snbchf.com/chf/chf-history/us-stagflation-chf/)
- The German mark also appreciated sharply (USD fell >20% vs. DEM in a single year, 1978); CHF declined "even further" per the SNB analysis

**2008 Global Financial Crisis:**
- USD/CHF: January 2008 ≈ 1.13; December 2008 ≈ 1.06
- Calendar year 2008: CHF appreciated approximately **+6.2% vs USD** on a year-end to year-end basis
- Source: [FreeCurrencyRates.com — USD/CHF 2008](https://freecurrencyrates.com/en/exchange-rate-history/USD-CHF/2008/cbr)
- Note: CHF appreciated more sharply during the Lehman panic (Sep-Oct 2008) before SNB intervention. Over the 3-4 years following the start of the GFC, CHF appreciated **~70%** vs. USD before SNB imposed the 1.20 EUR/CHF floor in September 2011.
- Source for multi-year figure: [Deutsche Bundesbank — Swiss franc and US dollar are safe-haven currencies](https://www.bundesbank.de/en/tasks/topics/swiss-franc-and-us-dollar-are-safe-haven-currencies-666534)

**2020 COVID crisis:**
- CHF maintained safe-haven demand; SNB intervened heavily to prevent excessive appreciation
- [UNVERIFIED specific % — SNB's FX intervention in 2020 totaled ~CHF 110 billion, indicating significant upward pressure that was administratively suppressed]

### Japanese Yen (JPY) as Safe Haven — 2008 GFC

- USD/JPY peaked around **124 in June 2007**
- Fell to **108 in August 2008**, then collapsed to **below 90 by year-end 2008**
- **Calendar year 2008: JPY appreciated ~18.7% vs. USD**
- Real exchange rate appreciation: over **+20%**
- Mechanism: unwinding of yen carry trades; repatriation of Japanese overseas assets during liquidity crisis
- Source: [IMF Working Paper WP/13/228 — The Curious Case of the Yen as a Safe Haven Currency](https://www.imf.org/external/pubs/ft/wp/2013/wp13228.pdf); [BIS Quarterly Review, 2009](https://www.bis.org/publ/qtrpdf/r_qt0912i.pdf)
