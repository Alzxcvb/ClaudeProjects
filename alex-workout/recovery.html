Recovery

Sauna • Cold Plunge • Nutrition • Sleep

Sauna Protocol

2-3x Per Week, Post-Workout

  • Duration: 15-20 minutes at ~80°C
  • Best timing: after Push or Pull days (upper body soreness)
  • Benefits: increased growth hormone, reduced DOMS, cardiovascular health
  • Hydrate before and after — bring water in with you

Cold Plunge Protocol

2-3x Per Week

  • Duration: 5-10 minutes, as cold as available
  • Best timing: morning (before training) OR on rest days
Do NOT cold plunge immediately after strength training. Cold kills the inflammation signal needed for muscle growth. Wait at least 4 hours, or save it for the next morning.
  • Exception: if you have significant pain/injury, cold plunge post-workout is fine
  • Focus on controlled breathing — in through nose, out through mouth

Contrast Therapy

Optional — Best on Sunday (Rest Day)

  • 10 min sauna → 5 min cold plunge → 10 min sauna → 5 min cold
  • Full-body recovery reset
  • Great for reducing weekly accumulated soreness

Nutrition

The Problem: Not Enough Total Calories

Current protein intake is strong (~140-200g/day). The gap is total calories — you need a surplus to build muscle.

Estimated maintenance ~2,200-2,400 cal/day
Target for muscle gain ~2,600-2,900 cal/day (+400-500 surplus)
Minimum protein ~115g/day (already exceeding this)

Practical Adjustments

  • Eat ALL of the rice at lunch + dinner — don't skip carbs
  • Add 1-2 extra eggs at breakfast if available
  • Take any snacks/fruit available between meals
  • Consider a protein shake post-workout (easy +25-30g protein)
  • No peanuts — use other nut sources if available
Simple rule: If the scale isn't going up ~0.5-1kg/month, eat more. If it's going up >2kg/month, ease back.

Sleep

Target: 8 Hours

  • Currently at 7 hrs — adequate, but 8 hrs is when muscle repair peaks
  • Growth hormone release is highest during deep sleep (hours 6-8)
  • Aim to align with 6-11 AM training window
  • Consistent sleep/wake times matter more than total hours
