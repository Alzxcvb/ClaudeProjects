Push Day

Chest • Shoulders • Triceps

Monday (Push A + Abs) & Thursday (Push B)

Rest Timer

01:30

Exercises

01
Barbell Overhead Press
4 × 8
Priority #1 — builds shoulder width. The single most important exercise for your "wide" goal.
Form Cues
  • Feet shoulder-width, slight lean back at the top
  • Bar starts at collarbone, press straight up
  • Push head through at lockout (ears in front of arms)
  • Brace core hard — no excessive arch
  • Control the descent, don't just drop it
02
Barbell Bench Press
4 × 8
Chest foundation. Start light — form matters more than ego weight.
Form Cues
  • Retract shoulder blades — pinch them together and down
  • Slight arch in upper back, feet flat on floor
  • Grip just outside shoulder width
  • Bar touches mid-chest, not neck or belly
  • Drive through feet, press up and slightly back
03
Incline Dumbbell Press
3 × 10
Upper chest development. 30-45 degree incline.
Form Cues
  • Bench at 30-45 degrees (not too steep)
  • Dumbbells start at shoulder height, palms forward
  • Press up and slightly together at top
  • Slow negative — 2-3 seconds down
04
Dumbbell Lateral Raises
4 × 12
Medial delts — creates the "wide shoulders" look. Go light, high reps.
Form Cues
  • Slight bend in elbows, keep them locked
  • Raise to shoulder height, not above
  • Lead with elbows, not hands
  • Pinky finger slightly higher than thumb (pour water)
  • Don't swing — if you need momentum, go lighter
05
Cable Tricep Pushdown
3 × 12
Tricep isolation. Use rope or bar attachment.
Form Cues
  • Elbows pinned to sides — only forearms move
  • Full lockout at bottom, squeeze tricep
  • Slow eccentric (2 sec up)
  • Don't lean over the cable
06
Overhead Tricep Extension
3 × 12
Long head tricep — the part that makes arms look big from the side.
Form Cues
  • Use one dumbbell with both hands, or cable
  • Keep elbows pointing forward, close to head
  • Lower behind head until deep stretch
  • Full lockout at top
